# Use Case Ideation Workshop

**Summary**: Half-day workshop methodology for generating a longlist of AI use case candidates from the client's own business processes.

**Last updated**: 2026-06-25

---

**Goal**: Generate a longlist of AI use cases relevant to the client's business, grounded in their actual processes and pain points — not a vendor feature list.

**Duration**: Half day (3–4 hours)
**Participants**: Business unit leads, process owners, operations managers — not IT or data teams yet. You want people who live with the problems, not people who think about solutions.

---

## Preparation (before the workshop)

- Do a 30-min briefing call with your main contact to understand the business context, priority areas, and what has already been tried
- Review publicly available information: annual report, competitor landscape, industry analyst reports on AI in this sector
- Prepare 3–5 concrete AI use case examples from the same industry (e.g., from [[catalog-manufacturing]]) as inspiration — not to anchor the group, but to make AI tangible for non-technical participants
- Prepare a simple use case capture template (problem, process, data hint, rough value) to fill in during the session

---

## Workshop structure

### 1. Scene setting (20 min)

- Explain what AI can and cannot do — anchor on the 3 capability types: predictive, generative, decision making
- Show 2–3 concrete industry examples to build intuition
- Clarify: you are not here to sell a specific technology, you are here to find problems worth solving

### 2. Process walk (60 min)

- Walk through the client's core end-to-end processes with the group (e.g., order-to-delivery, plan-to-produce, procure-to-pay)
- At each step, ask: *Where do people make decisions based on data? Where do things go wrong? Where is there waste, delay, or rework?*
- Capture pain points on a whiteboard or Miro, not use cases yet — stay problem-first
- Useful probing questions:
  - "What would you do differently if you had perfect information here?"
  - "What does your best expert know that a new hire doesn't?"
  - "Where do you spend time on tasks that feel like they should be automatic?"
  - "Where do quality issues or delays most often originate?"

### 3. Idea generation (45 min)

- **Now map pain points to potential AI capabilities** — do this with the group, not for them
- **For each pain point: could this be a prediction problem? An automation problem? A content/document problem?**
- Write each raw idea on a sticky note / card: one sentence, problem + proposed AI action
- Quantity over quality at this stage — 20–30 raw ideas is a good output

### 4. Initial clustering and scoring (30 min)

- Group ideas into themes (quality, planning, maintenance, procurement, etc.)
- Do a quick gut-check scoring with the group on two axes:
  - **Business value**: low / medium / high (revenue impact, cost reduction, risk reduction)
  - **AI feasibility intuition**: does data likely exist? Is the problem well-defined?
- Goal: identify a shortlist of 5–8 ideas worth taking into the feasibility assessment
- Do not commit to anything yet — this is directional, not a decision

### 5. Wrap-up (15 min)

- Present the shortlist back to the group
- Agree on the 5–8 use cases to assess in detail
- Identify the right people to involve in the feasibility workshop (data owners, IT, domain experts)

---

## Output

A shortlist of 5–8 prioritized use case candidates, each described as:
- Business problem (1–2 sentences)
- Process step where it occurs
- Rough business value hypothesis
- Data availability hypothesis (gut feel only at this stage)
