**The MVP should be designed as a structured, quasi-deterministic workflow in which the overall process logic remains explicit and controllable. The agent should not be understood as one large autonomous system that freely decides how to process an offer request from end to end. Instead, the workflow should define the sequence of steps, required inputs, validation points, user confirmations, and system integrations. LLM capabilities are then inserted selectively where language understanding or interpretation is required.**


**

## Why workflow automation is the smarter starting point for DEALiX, not the compromise?

When a process is well understood, workflow automation is not the simpler fallback. It is the more appropriate tool.

  

Agentic AI is powerful precisely because it does not need the process to be pre-defined, the agent figures it out. But that strength becomes a liability when the process is already known and agreed. Asking an agent to reason its way through a process that has been mapped, documented, and validated by the business introduces unpredictability where none is needed. It also makes it significantly harder to audit, debug, and improve.
